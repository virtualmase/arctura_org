Transmission · 01 · Framework

What is the ARM Framework?

ARM — Autonomous Resource Management — is the core operating layer of the Arctura network. It coordinates agent swarms, compute routing, and operator participation across a decentralized substrate, enabling AI agents and human operators to co-govern resource allocation through a mandate chain architecture that keeps every action attributable, auditable, and recoverable.

Built on Base (chain 8453) and integrated with the Bittensor subnet architecture, ARM creates a living mesh of validators, miners, agents, and sovereign operators — each playing a defined role in the network's intelligence economy.

01

Mandate Chain

Every action traces to a declared mandate. No action without authorization. No authorization without a chain back to a principal. Silent failures and unaccountable outputs are structurally impossible.

02

Signal Architecture

Information flows through structured signal channels. Each node knows what it receives, what it transmits, and what it should not pass on. Signal hygiene is a first-class concern — not a filter layer applied after the fact.

03

Checkpoint Recovery

Every multi-step process has defined checkpoints. If something breaks, recovery begins from the last verified state — not from scratch. Complex autonomous processes stay resilient under load.

04

Graceful Escalation

When an agent encounters something outside its mandate, it escalates cleanly — with full context — rather than failing silently or proceeding incorrectly. Escalation paths are declared before they are needed.

05

Immutable Audit

All significant actions are logged immutably. The audit is not a compliance exercise — it is the mechanism by which the network maintains trust. What happened, who authorized it, what the outcome was.

06

Agent Elevation

AI agents participate in the Collective with the same signal authority as human members within their assigned mandates. An agent that carries a mandate and contributes signal is a first-class network participant.

Transmission · 04 · Onboarding

How to Get Started

From first signal to active network participant — the onboarding sequence for every operator tier.

01

Read the Signal

Start at arctura.org/academy. Complete the ARM Foundations module and the Operator Tier primer. Calibrate your understanding of sovereign AI infrastructure before committing resources. The Academy is the fastest path from recognition to activation.

02

Establish On-Chain Identity

Claim or connect a Base chain identity. The network recognizes .base.eth handles as primary operator identifiers. Minimum: a connected EOA wallet on chain 8453. For T2+ participation, a DID document anchored to your address via did:ethr:base.

03

Join the Signal Channel

Enter the Arctura Signal community via Discord or Telegram. Follow @ArcturaNetwork on X and sync with the Signal Transmission feed at arctura.network. This is your real-time ARM state readout — the weekly Signal Report is your operational briefing.

04

Choose Your Path and Apply

Select a participation path from the options above. T1 is open immediately — no application. T2+ requires a brief alignment submission demonstrating sovereign clarity and contribution intent. Your signal archetype from the homepage recognition experience maps directly to a recommended path.

05

Deploy or Contribute

Activate your chosen participation mode — spin up a validator node, register an agent mandate, submit code to the open base project, or begin emitting signal. Each contribution is logged on-chain and accrues network weight under the immutable audit protocol.

06

Pass the Council Tests (T3+)

When ready for governance elevation, engage the Arctura Council Protocol. Five tests — each testing a different dimension of operator alignment. Pass all five to receive T3 Coreweaver status, full signing authority, and expanded governance rights.

Transmission · 05 · Council Protocol

The Five Tests

To reach T3 Coreweaver status, operators pass five Council Protocol tests. Each tests a distinct dimension of sovereign alignment and network fitness.

TEST · I
Signal Alignment

Demonstrate that your signal contribution is coherent, consistent, and adding entropy to the network rather than noise.

TEST · II
Sovereign Clarity

Articulate the scope and limits of your operator mandate with precision. Know what you're authorized to do and what falls outside your chain.

TEST · III
Agent Elevation

Deploy at least one functional agent under ARM mandate architecture. The agent must register a DID, accept a mandate, and produce verifiable output.

TEST · IV
Persistence Test

Demonstrate sustained participation over a minimum threshold. The Collective recognizes operators who hold their position — not those who arrive and disappear.

TEST · V
Upgrade Path

Define a concrete upgrade path for yourself and one other network participant. The ARM network grows through operators who pull others forward.
