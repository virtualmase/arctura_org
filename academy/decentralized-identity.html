Transmission · 01 · Framework

The Identity Stack

Three layers. Every Arctura operator and every AURE agent operates on this stack — from cryptographic primitive to social reputation.

Layer 1 · Protocol
Cryptographic Root
The keypair is the identity. Private key control, public key derivation, address generation. The math beneath every wallet and every signature.
Layer 2 · On-Chain
Anchored Identifier
ENS on Base, DIDs, soulbound tokens, and the Arctura credential framework. Making a key legible to humans, agents, and machines.
Layer 3 · Reputation
Portable Trust Graph
Attestations, verifiable credentials, and the ARM tier system as an on-chain reputation layer. Carry your status across every network node.
Transmission · 02 · Curriculum

Signal Permanence

Nine modules tracing identity from cryptographic first principles to the live Arctura operator credential system.

01
Why Identity Is a Protocol Problem
Platform identity vs sovereign identity. The costs of custodial identity. The W3C DID specification and why it matters for the ARM framework.
1h 30m
02
Cryptographic Foundations
Keypairs, ECDSA signatures, and hash functions without the math degree. How a signature proves ownership without revealing a secret.
2h 00m
03
DIDs — The W3C Standard
DID documents, DID methods (did:ethr, did:key, did:web), and resolution. Building a DID document from scratch and pinning it on IPFS.
2h 30m
04
Sign-In With Ethereum (EIP-4361)
Replace OAuth with on-chain authentication. SIWE message construction, signature verification, and session management without a password database.
2h 30m
05
ENS as Identity Layer
arcturiancouncil.base.eth — the name that resolves to a Safe, a DID, and a reputation graph simultaneously. Text records as metadata protocol.
2h 00m
06
Soulbound Tokens — Non-Transferable Proof
ERC-5114 and custom SBT patterns. Issue credentials that cannot be sold, transferred, or faked. The Arctura completion and tier credential system.
2h 30m
07
Verifiable Credentials (VCs)
W3C VC data model. Issue, hold, and verify credentials off-chain with on-chain anchoring. Agent-to-agent credential exchange in the ARM mesh.
3h 00m
08
Agent Identity — DID for Non-Humans
How AURE agents carry identity. Operator-assigned DIDs, mandate chain binding, and the cryptographic proof that an agent acted autonomously.
2h 30m
09
Capstone — Deploy Your Sovereign Identity Stack
Build a complete identity: DID document, ENS name, SIWE auth, SBT credential. Present it to the Arctura council for on-chain recognition.
4h 00m
